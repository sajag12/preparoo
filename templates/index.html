Your CAT prep doesn't have to be a
mystery.

Preparing for CAT can feel like shouting into the void. We've been there. So, we broke down our own prep into a simple, repeatable cycle that turned confusion into confidence. Here's how you can use it too.

Step 1

Take High-Quality Mocks

Start with a realistic test. Our mocks are made to feel like the actual CAT, helping you build the stamina and focus you need for D-day.

  • Take on 15+ full-length mocks that mirror the actual CAT environment.
  • Our questions are thoughtfully created after analyzing every CAT paper from 2017-2024.
  • We cover the different types of recurring questions, so you're never caught off guard.
  • Feeling short on time? Drill down with 30+ targeted sectional tests.
Step 2

Analyze Your Performance (The Right Way)

Your score is just the beginning. We help you go beyond that number to understand the why behind every click.

  • Forget just 'right' or 'wrong'; our analytics show you why you made a mistake.
  • Use the Question Heatmap to see your timing, from "quick correct" to "long incorrect."
  • Analyze your question-picking strategy. Did you miss easy questions (missed opportunities)?
  • Identify time-wasters: difficult questions you spent too long on and should have skipped.
Step 3

Pinpoint Your Weaknesses & Improve

Get a crystal-clear roadmap of what to work on next. Our analysis turns vague feelings of weakness into a specific, actionable study plan.

  • Our SWOT analysis gives you an honest look at your strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Go from "I'm weak in Quants" to knowing "I need to work on Arithmetic."
  • Drill down even further, from "Arithmetic" to a specific sub-topic like "Trains."
  • We even point out specific patterns, like the "trains speed change after accident" question type.
Step 4

Practice with Purpose (and Repeat)

Now, you turn those insights into strengths. This is how you close the gaps in your knowledge and strategy, one focused session at a time.

  • Jump into our 30+ sectional tests, built to help you conquer your identified weak areas.
  • Practice with questions and patterns seen in the CAT from 2017-2024.
  • With every practice session, you close a gap. Then, you take another mock and repeat the cycle.
  • This is how small, consistent efforts lead to big percentile jumps.
